About Us

The State Department of Motor Vehicles (DMV) is a critical public sector agency dedicated to providing essential services across our state. These services include driver's license renewals, vehicle registration, appointment scheduling for road tests, and payment processing. Our core mission is to ensure efficient, secure, and accessible services for all residents, upholding public safety and convenience.

As citizen expectations for modern, intuitive self-service continue to evolve, we are committed to leveraging cutting-edge technology to transform our service delivery. This includes reducing wait times, streamlining processes, and significantly improving overall citizen satisfaction.

Our Network & Operations

  • Scope: Our operations encompass one central statewide contact center supported by 8 regional branch offices strategically located across the state.
  • Agents: We employ approximately 450 dedicated contact center agents, comprising a mix of full-time and part-time staff, who handle a high volume of citizen inquiries.
  • Key Services: Citizens interact with us for a variety of critical services, including:
    • License renewals and replacements
    • Vehicle registration and title services
    • Scheduling and managing appointments (e.g., road tests, in-person services)
    • Processing payments for various DMV services
    • General inquiries and information dissemination
  • Citizen Interactions: We manage a consistently high volume of citizen interactions, with significant peak periods occurring around month-end and fiscal year-end, which often lead to increased wait times.

Current Communication Systems

Our existing technology infrastructure, while functional, presents challenges in meeting modern citizen expectations and state IT mandates:

  • Contact Center Platform: We currently operate on UCCX 12.5 on-premise, which relies on End-of-Life (EOL) servers and features aging, complex IVR scripts that are difficult to modify quickly.
  • Telephony Infrastructure: Our core telephony is managed by CUCM 11.5, with Jabber utilized by supervisors for communication.
  • Branch Office PBXs: Regional branches still utilize legacy Avaya PBX systems, leading to fragmented communication.
  • Call Recording & Analytics: We use NICE for call recording, but its integration is clunky, offering limited analytics and reporting capabilities.
  • Channels: Our primary interaction channel is voice, with very limited self-service options, contributing to high call volumes for simple tasks.
  • Challenges: The complexity of UCCX scripting makes rapid menu changes difficult, directly contributing to long wait times and citizen frustration. The reliance on legacy hardware and fragmented systems across our network hinders efficient, unified service delivery.

Modernization Vision

Our strategic vision is to transition to a modern, cloud-native platform, in full alignment with the state's overarching cloud-first IT policy. This pivotal move aims to eliminate our reliance on aging on-premise hardware and the complexities of legacy scripting, thereby enabling greater operational agility and significantly improved service delivery.

  • Core Goal: Migrate from our current UCCX environment to Webex Contact Center (WxCC), establishing a unified, scalable, and highly agile platform for all citizen services.
  • Strategic Approach: Embrace AI-driven self-service capabilities and intelligent agent assistance tools to optimize contact center operations and profoundly enhance the citizen experience.
  • Anticipated Outcome: The deployment of a modern, resilient platform that delivers faster resolution times, substantially reduced operational overhead, and a marked increase in overall citizen satisfaction scores.

What We’re Solving For (Key Drivers & Benefits)

Our modernization initiative is directly addressing several critical pain points and is driven by clear business objectives:

  • Reduce Wait Times: By implementing AI-driven self-service, we aim to deflect 30-40% of repetitive inquiries such as license renewals, appointment rescheduling, and basic FAQ, significantly shortening citizen hold times.
  • Improve Agent Efficiency: Equipping agents with AI Assistant tools will provide immediate call context and access to knowledge bases, reducing Average Handle Time (AHT) and accelerating agent training.
  • Enhance Operational Agility: Moving to a cloud platform will simplify IVR script updates, transforming a process that currently takes months into mere minutes, allowing us to respond rapidly to policy changes or evolving service needs.
  • Increase Citizen Satisfaction: Offering modern self-service options and ensuring seamless agent transfers with full context will reduce citizen frustration and lead to higher satisfaction scores.
  • Minimize Infrastructure Overhead: Retiring EOL servers and eliminating on-premise licenses will result in substantial cost savings and a reduced IT operational burden.

Leadership Team (Key Stakeholders)

Our modernization efforts are championed by a dedicated team of leaders:

  • CIO: Spearheading the cloud-first mandate, ensuring robust security, and seamless integration with broader state IT systems.
  • Contact Center Director: Focused on dramatically improving Citizen Experience (CX), reducing Average Handle Time (AHT), and consistently meeting Service Level Agreements (SLAs).
  • Agent Supervisor: Committed to reducing agent training time and empowering our agents to confidently handle even the most complex inquiries.
  • IT Operations Lead: Tasked with minimizing infrastructure overhead, simplifying system upgrades, and ensuring operational stability.
